I am currently a fifth-year Ph.D. student at the Department of Astronomy, Tsinghua University, supervised by Prof. Zheng Cai (蔡峥). I am an observer and focus on the galaxies and cosmic interstellar/circumgalactic/intergalactic in the early universe.

Research

The identification of the host systems of metal absorbers

Using the ALMA and JWST data, we construct the sample of early metals and their hosts (e.g., z>2) to study the correlation between metals and galactic properties and understand the baryonic cycle in the early universe.

Properties of dusty galaxies

In the JWST ear, we are now able to observe the rest-frame optical emission from dust galaxies (submillimeter galaxies, SMGs). We dsicovered the most distant spiral galaxies ever found.
